About

Splice Check is a tool designed to check if a missense mutation causes a splice defect that could potentially be ammenable by antisense oligonucleotide therapy. Typically missense mutations are assumed to be pathogenic because of the impact that single amino acid change has on the protein structure, but in many cases the nucleotide change could be altering splicing by destroying a canonical splice site or creating a new one. We work in a genetics lab at Boston Children's Hospital and we are working to fix this splicing mutations in children with rare diseases using a type of personalized gene therapy. Read more about that here.

References

Splice Check calls the following tools and uses them for this analysis.

VEP: Variant Effect Predictor SIFT: A SIFT score predicts whether an amino acid substitution affects protein function by looking at how well it is conserved across different species. PolyPhen-2: This tool predicts possible impact of an amino acid substitution on the structure and function of a human protein. MaxEntScan: MaxEntScan is a splice predicting algorithm. OMIM: Online Mendelian Inheritance in Man is a large database of human genetic resources. gnomad: gnomAD is a genomic database coprised of 125,748 exome sequences and 15,708 whole-genome sequences from unrelated individuals sequenced as part of various disease-specific and population genetic studies.
